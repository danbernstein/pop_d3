Pencils of Promise
Pencils of Promise builds schools in Laos, Nicaragua, Guatemala, and Ghana, and trains teachers to improve education systems around the world.

Scroll through to learn about a subset of their teachers.
Teachers in different grade
Teachers are assigned to classrooms in elementary, middle, and high school. In this subset, we are looking at students in grades 1-6.
Evaluating teacher performance improvements.
Pencils of Promise is conducting a randomized control trial to understand if their intervention improves teacher performance on rubrics.
Overall Performance
The teachers are evaluated on eleven criteria, on a scale of 1 to 4 for each question, for a maximum of 44 points.
In the beginning...
The control and treatment groups have similar overall evaluation scores in the beginning. The treatment group is less than one point higher than the control.
After intervention
But after the training intervention, the treatment group scored over three points higher than the control overall.
Communication with Students
In one area, teachers were assessed for their communication with students, based on two questions: to what levels are directions clearly delivered and understandable? and To what level is content communicated in a clear, concise manner?

Big Improvements
Teachers in the treatment group leaped passed their peers, jumping from 0.2 points lower before the intervention to half a point better afterwards!
Creating an Environment of Respect and Rapport
Teachers were also evaluated on questions such as to what level are interactions in the classroom positive and productive?
Creating an Environment of Respect and Rapport
Teachers in the treatment group made big gains here too! Improving from 0.2 points lower than the control group to 0.2 points ahead!
